Industry Dynamics & Key Shifts

Daliatime Chapter 1 was not just about music; it was a period of significant change. Album sales experienced a noticeable dip, while the industry's landscape became more polarized, with major agencies solidifying their dominance. Amid these shifts, nostalgic trends like group reunions and the rise of new genres took hold.

Genre Snapshot

Pop: The foundational genre, evolving with modern twists. Subgenres like UK Garage (UKG) and Miami Bass became popular, with artists experimenting with new sounds.
Example: `Like Magic` (M.O.N.T), `Bubble Gum` (NewJeans), `Magnetic` (ILLIT).

R&B/Soul: A constant presence, often fused with modern beats and smooth vocals.
Example: `Only Cry in the Rain` (Chuu), `Elevator` (BAEKHYUN), `SoÃ±ar (Breaker)` (NMIXX).

Hip-Hop: Both traditional and experimental forms saw success, with artists showcasing their rapping and production skills.
Example: `Chk Chk Boom` (Stray Kids), `Spot!` (ZICO ft. JENNIE), `Hate to Love` (Jackson Wang).

Concept Snapshot

Y2K Nostalgia: This concept, pioneered by groups like NewJeans, dominated the era. It blends late '90s and early 2000s aesthetics, sounds, and fashion, creating a sense of comforting retro familiarity.
Example: NewJeans' entire visual and sonic identity.

Futuristic/AI: A concept that explores technology and a hyper-real universe, often accompanied by complex lore and visual effects.
Example: aespa's `Supernova` and PLAVE's digital identity.

Girl Crush: A confident, powerful, and often edgy concept that focuses on female empowerment and self-love.
Example: (G)I-DLE's `Super Lady` and LE SSERAFIM's `Easy`, `DASH` (NMIXX).

Significant Industry Events

  • Global Festival Participation: Major K-Pop acts, including ATEEZ, Stray Kids, and LE SSERAFIM, made waves with headlining performances at global festivals like Coachella, solidifying K-Pop's mainstream presence.
  • NewJeans Legal Disputes: An ongoing legal battle involving the group's management label, ADOR, and parent company HYBE dominated headlines, highlighting tensions within the industry.
  • Group Disbandments: The dissolution of veteran groups like Cherry Bullet and Weki Meki marked the end of an era for many fans, shifting the landscape.
  • Virtual Artist Agency: The formation of dedicated agencies and labels for virtual idols, such as VLAST for PLAVE, signaled a growing investment in the genre.
